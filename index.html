San Juan Batangas


Local specialties and time-honored traditional dishes that showcase the town's unique flavors, ingredients, and culinary traditions may be found in San Juan, Batangas. The following list of famous San Juan dishes is ideal for anyone wishing to learn more about Batangueño culture through its food:
Name Picture Description
Sinukmani Latik, which means two different coconut-based ingredients or cooked coconut curds, are placed on top of a delicious rice cake prepared with glutinous rice and coconut milk. A popular treat that in San Juan that is frequently served during fiestas, community gatherings, and special occasions.
Lomi Batangas Known for its thick, starchy broth, Batangas Lomi is a local noodle dish that stole the hearts of many with its unique and delicious taste. This dish is ideal for a quick and fulfilling supper in the area because it is loaded with pork liver, kikiam, fish balls, and occasionally chicharrón and hard-boiled eggs.
Gotong Batangas Gotong Batangas is a special kind of Filipino goto (porridge) that doesn't contain rice. It's a flavorful, mildly tart soup made with liver and bovine intestines that has been seasoned with vinegar and garlic. This dish is frequently offered during local gatherings and is considered to be savory and delicious.
Tapang Taal Another Batangueño favorite, Tapang Taal is marinated beef that's slightly salty, garlicky, and tender. It’s often served with garlic rice, fried egg, and vinegar dipping sauce, making it a popular breakfast or lunch dish for locals and visitors.
Adobo sa Dilaw A unique version of adobo found in Batangas, this dish uses ginger, giving it a vibrant yellow color and earthy taste.Typically made with chicken or pork, it offers a unique flavor signature compared to the usual soy sauce-based adobo.
